The Process

MindHaven

A mental health web application prototype that aids individuals in finding personalized solutions that can aid with anxiety and stress.

October 2023 - December 2023

The Problem

Many individuals aged between 18 - 30 years old do not seek out mental health aid.

"Young adults ages 18 to 25 in the U.S have the highest rate of experiencing any mental health concerns (33.7%) compared to adults aged 26 to 49 years, and the highest rate of serious mental illness (11.4%)"

-Forbes Health

Our Solution

MindHaven strives to be an innovative website designed to offer college students and young professionals resources to understand and manage their stress and mental health. It combines educational content with access to professional mental health support, creating a supportive community for navigating the unique challenges of academic and early career pressures.

Our Target Clients

As a team we created Assumptive Personas for the client we wanted to target.

Main Points:

  • Age: This was a very important factor for us and we chose to target those who fall in the age range of 18-25 years old. Many times those in this age range either don't have the motivation or the funding to get aid. Our goals were to help those who may not be well-versed in mental health aid and don't know where to start.
  • Occupation:We targeted those who either were still university students or just started a full-time job. Due to the cost of mental health aid many of these individuals in this age range tend to avoid seeking help. Our goal was to make a platform that was efficient to use and cost-free to encourage alternative ways of help.

Our Approach

Our goal was to make an efficient platform that had useful resources.

  • Navigation: We created five main pages that were: Discover, Community, Resources, About, and My Profile. The Discover page is the official main page of the platform, giving the user a glimpse of the entire application. The Community is a way for the users to connect with others, give them a chance to share their own stories and to have a social component to the application. The Reources is the main page for users to explore tools that the platform has to offer, the About is an introduction to our team and mission statement, and lastly the My Profile allows the users to maintain their profile.
  • Wireframing:There were a lot of resources we wanted to implement and we had numerous stages of wireframing and drafting. Due to certain pages having to rely on an actual socialization after the middle fidelity stage we ended up focusing mainly on our Discover and Resources Pages. The goal was to contain all the information without overwhelming our users. We also wanted to emphasize the ability to take an assesment for a personalized solution."
  • Usability Testing:After creating a working prototype of our Discover and Resources pages we began to do Usability testing. We used snowball sampling to gather people interested in testing the product. We created a protocol that contained charts to help ensure that important components were functioning properly. After this testing we put together a presentation of key findings and possible fixes. The biggest findings were the length of informaiton on the Discover Page and also the navigation bar as it was having some glitch issues.

The Key Changes

  • Removing Distracting ElementsIn the first few phases of wireframing we had a lot of content we wanted to showcase. Because of the amount of content some of the indivudlas who did our usability testing stated that it was a bit overwhelming. Therefore, we decided to scale down on the amount of information and create a layout that still showcases various componenets but doesn't overwhelm the user.
  • Breadcrumbs:Understanding that our platform would have various pages, during our lowfi and midfi wireframes we didn't consider utilizing breadcrumbs in navigation. However, by the final wireframe we decided it would help to keep users informed of where they are on the applicaiton at all times. Allowing our users to feel control over their navigation.

This would allow them to feel a sense of community on our platform.
